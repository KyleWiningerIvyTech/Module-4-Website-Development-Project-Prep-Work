A STEALTHY APPROACH
During a time when mascot Platformers were going out of style, a studio by the name of “Suckerpunch” hatched a scheme that would allow them to stay relevant during this transition. it involved a young, clever raccoon that would prove to be a hit with sony fans across the world. Bold FORMULATING A PLAN Bold After releasing their first game, Rocket Robot on Wheels, Suckerpunch quickly found themselves struggling to create an IP that would stick with audiences. With a character design that many considered to be uninspired, along with the rest of the game following suit, Suckerpunch needed a new plan, and fast.

Sly Cooper was intially envisioned as being a lot more accurate to a real life raccoon, complete with big proportions. This design would later be overhauled to appear more sleek and agile.

Going back to the drawing board, the team very quickly came up with the idea to create a game where the world and characters looked cartoonish, but with a touch of realism to them as well. This is how they came up with the concept for the character, Sly Cooper.

A STRONG FIRST IMPRESSION


Sly Cooper and The Thievius Raccoonus was released in September 24, 2002.

Sly Cooper would go on to sell around 800,000 copies in total. Despite this however, in order for Suckerpunch to stay in business, they would need to once again go back to the drawing board. With the knowledge they had gained from working on Sly Cooper, they would immediately begin development on a sequel.



ADAPTING TO THE TIMES



Sly 2 Band of Thieves was released in September 14, 2004.

Sly 2 retained a lot of what made Sly 1 memorable. What is new in Sly 2 is its more open structure. The developers drew inspiration from Disneyland with the world design, allowing the player to complete jobs that build towards a big heist at the end of each episode. These changes were welcomed by fans and critics alike.

Sly 2 was a step up from Sly 1 in the sales department, selling around 1.2 million copies and providing Suckerpunch the necessary funds to stay afloat for the forseeable future. With this success, Suckerpunch would begin development on one last Sly game. Only this time, it would be a more enjoyable challenge for the team.



ENDING ON A HIGH NOTE



Sly 3 Honor Among Thieves was released in September 26, 2005.

Thanks to how well Sly 2 performed, the dev team had much more confidence going in to Sly 3. Instead of trying to survive as a studio, they could instead go all out with Sly 3, filling it with all kinds of mini games and extra polish. Fans and critics welcomed the extra variety to the gameplay in this final entry.

Sly 3 once again sold around 1.2 million copies, recapturing the glory of Sly 2 and allowing Suckerpunch to confidently leave the series behind and move on to a new IP. Despite being done with the franchise however, they left a small cliffhanger in Sly 3 so that another developer could take the reins, if given the chance.
