A STEALTHY APPROACH

During a time when mascot Platformers were going out of style, a studio by the name of “Suckerpunch” hatched a scheme that would allow them to stay relevant during this transition. it involved a young, clever raccoon that would prove to be a hit with sony fans across the world.

FORMULATING A PLAN



After releasing their first game, Rocket Robot on Wheels, Suckerpunch quickly found themselves struggling to create an IP that would stick with audiences. With a character design that many considered to be uninspired, along with the rest of the game following suit, Suckerpunch needed a new plan, and fast.





Sly Cooper was intially envisioned as being a lot more accurate to a real life raccoon, complete with big proportions. This design would later be overhauled to appear more sleek and agile.

Going back to the drawing board, the team very quickly came up with the idea to create a game where the world and characters looked cartoonish, but with a touch of realism to them as well. This is how they came up with the concept for the character, Sly Cooper.
